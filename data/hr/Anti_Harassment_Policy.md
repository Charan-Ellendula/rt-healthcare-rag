# Anti-Harassment & Anti-Discrimination Policy
Company: RT Healthcare
Department: Human Resources
Policy Owner: Chief Human Resources Officer
Effective Date: [Insert Date]
Version: 3.0
Classification: Internal

---

## 1. Policy Statement

RT Healthcare is committed to providing a work environment free from unlawful harassment, discrimination, retaliation, and inappropriate conduct. 

Harassment of any kind—whether verbal, physical, visual, digital, or psychological—is strictly prohibited and will not be tolerated.

This policy is designed to comply with:
- Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA)
- Pregnancy Discrimination Act
- Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA)
- Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) guidance
- Applicable state and local employment laws

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All full-time and part-time employees
- Temporary employees and interns
- Contractors and consultants
- Volunteers
- Vendors and third parties interacting with RT Healthcare

This policy applies to conduct occurring:
- On company premises
- At healthcare facilities
- During remote work
- At company-sponsored events
- During business travel
- Through electronic communication systems

---

## 3. Prohibited Conduct

Harassment is unwelcome conduct based on protected characteristics including:

- Race
- Color
- Religion
- Sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, gender identity)
- National origin
- Age (40+)
- Disability
- Genetic information
- Veteran status
- Any protected category under applicable law

---

## 4. Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment includes:

### A. Quid Pro Quo
When submission to sexual conduct is made a condition of employment.

### B. Hostile Work Environment
Conduct that is severe or pervasive enough to create an intimidating or offensive workplace.

Examples:
- Unwelcome sexual advances
- Requests for sexual favors
- Sexually explicit jokes or comments
- Display of inappropriate materials
- Unwanted physical contact
- Repeated romantic advances after rejection

---

## 5. Other Forms of Harassment

Includes:
- Racial slurs or stereotypes
- Mocking religious practices
- Age-related derogatory remarks
- Disability-based exclusion
- Cultural insults
- Cyberbullying or online harassment

---

## 6. Reporting Procedure

Employees must report incidents promptly.

Reports may be made to:
- Direct supervisor
- Any manager
- Human Resources
- Compliance Officer
- Anonymous Ethics Hotline (if available)

Employees are not required to report to a supervisor involved in the complaint.

---

## 7. Investigation Process

RT Healthcare will:

1. Acknowledge complaint within 3 business days
2. Assign trained investigator
3. Conduct confidential interviews
4. Collect documentation and digital evidence
5. Apply "preponderance of evidence" standard
6. Conclude investigation in a timely manner (typically within 30 days)

---

## 8. Interim Protective Measures

Where necessary, interim steps may include:
- Temporary reassignment
- Administrative leave
- Separation of involved parties
- Work schedule adjustments

---

## 9. Non-Retaliation

Retaliation is strictly prohibited.

Prohibited retaliation includes:
- Termination
- Demotion
- Reduced hours
- Negative evaluations
- Harassment for reporting

Any retaliation will result in disciplinary action.

---

## 10. Disciplinary Action

Violations may result in:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Suspension
- Demotion
- Termination
- Termination of vendor contract

---

## 11. Training Requirements

Mandatory annual training includes:
- Harassment prevention
- Bystander intervention
- Leadership accountability

Managers receive enhanced training on complaint handling.

---

## 12. Documentation & Record Retention

All complaints and investigations will be documented and retained in accordance with legal retention requirements (minimum 7 years unless otherwise required by law).

---

## 13. Policy Review

Reviewed annually or upon regulatory change.
