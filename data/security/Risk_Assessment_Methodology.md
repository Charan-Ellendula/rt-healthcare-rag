# 1. Purpose

This document establishes the formal methodology for conducting enterprise-wide risk assessments at RT Healthcare. The objective is to identify, evaluate, prioritize, and treat risks that may impact confidentiality, integrity, availability, regulatory compliance, financial stability, and operational continuity.

This methodology aligns with:

- NIST SP 800-30 (Risk Assessment Guide)
- ISO/IEC 27005 (Information Security Risk Management)
- HIPAA Security Rule Risk Analysis Requirement
- SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria

---

# 2. Scope

This methodology applies to:

- Information systems and applications
- Cloud infrastructure
- Network components
- Endpoints and devices
- Third-party services
- Business processes
- Physical facilities
- Human factors

---

# 3. Risk Governance Structure

Risk oversight is governed by:

- Executive Risk Committee
- Chief Compliance Officer
- CISO
- Internal Audit
- System Owners

---

# 4. Risk Assessment Lifecycle

## 4.1 Asset Identification

All assets must be cataloged including:

- Hardware
- Software
- Databases
- APIs
- AI models
- Business processes
- Data repositories
- Third-party integrations

Each asset must include:
- Owner
- Classification
- Business criticality
- Data sensitivity level

---

## 4.2 Threat Identification

Threat sources include:

- Cybercriminal actors
- Insider threats
- Nation-state actors
- Human error
- System failure
- Natural disasters

Threat intelligence sources:
- Industry alerts
- Government advisories
- Threat intelligence feeds

---

## 4.3 Vulnerability Identification

Methods include:

- Vulnerability scans
- Penetration testing
- Configuration reviews
- Code review findings
- Audit findings
- Incident postmortems

---

## 4.4 Risk Analysis

Risk is calculated using:

Risk = Likelihood Ã— Impact

### Likelihood Criteria
- Rare
- Unlikely
- Possible
- Likely
- Almost Certain

### Impact Criteria
- Insignificant
- Minor
- Moderate
- Major
- Catastrophic

Impact must consider:
- Regulatory penalties
- Financial loss
- Operational downtime
- Reputational damage
- Patient safety impact

---

## 4.5 Risk Treatment Options

- Mitigate
- Transfer
- Accept
- Avoid

All risk acceptance decisions require documented executive approval.

---

# 5. Risk Register Requirements

Each risk must document:

- Risk ID
- Description
- Category
- Inherent Risk Score
- Control Effectiveness
- Residual Risk Score
- Owner
- Target Remediation Date
- Status

---

# 6. Review Frequency

Risk assessments must be conducted:

- Annually
- After major system changes
- After security incidents
- Upon regulatory changes

---

# 7. Documentation & Retention

Risk assessment documentation must be retained for a minimum of 7 years.
